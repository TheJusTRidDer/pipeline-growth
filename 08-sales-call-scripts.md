# Sales Call Script Book

---

## Script 1: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

**Purpose:** Diagnose pipeline, build value, decide next step
**Best for:** New prospects who replied to outreach

---

**OPENING (0-2 min)**

"Hi [Name], thanks for the time. I know you're busy so I'll be direct.

I help professional services firms build predictable pipeline systems — specifically firms like yours that rely too much on referrals and want more control over their growth.

To make this call valuable for you: what's your current growth situation? What's working, what's not?"

---

**DIAGNOSIS (2-12 min)**

*Goal: Understand their pipeline, pain, and willingness to change*

"Walk me through how you get new clients today. What are your top 3 sources of leads?"

[Listen. Note referral dependency, website, networking, etc.]

"Out of those, which one is most reliable? Which one do you wish was more reliable?"

"What happens when you have a slow month? How do you recover?"

"Have you ever invested in any kind of sales or growth support before?"

— If yes: "What did you try? What worked? What didn't?"

— If no: "What's held you back from trying?"

"What's your close rate on proposals? Rough is fine."

"How many proposals did you send last month? How many closed?"

"What's the average deal size for a new client engagement?"

---

**PAINTING THE PROBLEM (12-17 min)**

*Goal: Make the cost of inaction tangible*

"Let me make sure I understand your situation correctly:"

[Summarize their answers]

"You're currently getting [X]% of business from referrals. That means [X]% of your revenue depends on people you already know.

The challenge is: referrals cap out. You can only know so many people. And when referrals slow down — which they always do eventually — you have no backup system.

The firms I work with typically have this pattern:
• 60-80% referral dependent
• Feast or famine revenue
• No systematic outbound process
• 30-50% lower close rate than they could have with proper follow-up

Does that resonate?"

[Listen for agreement. If they resist, dig deeper: "Tell me more about why that doesn't fit."]

---

**VALUE DEMONSTRATION (17-22 min)**

*Goal: Show them a clear path to a different future*

"Here's what I've seen work for firms like yours:"

[Share 1 relevant case study — anonymized if needed]

"[Similar Firm] was in a similar position — [X]% referral dependent, slow quarters were brutal.

In 90 days, we:
• Built a targeted prospect list of 200+ ideal clients
• Launched a systematic email + LinkedIn outreach cadence
• Set up a simple pipeline tracking system

Result: 40% more pipeline in 60 days, 3 new clients in 90 days.

The key wasn't anything fancy — it was having a system that runs consistently instead of relying on bursts of effort."

"Does that kind of outcome sound like something that would be valuable to you?"

---

**OFFER (22-25 min)**

*Option A: If they seem ready for ongoing work*

"I think we could get you similar results. Here's what I'd recommend:

Start with a Growth Engine retainer at $3,500/month. That includes:
• A full pipeline audit
• Ongoing prospect list building (50+ targets/month)
• Cold email sequence management
• LinkedIn strategy
• Bi-weekly calls
• Pipeline dashboard

If you don't see measurable pipeline improvement in 60 days, cancel and pay nothing for your final month.

Does that make sense given what we've discussed?"

*Option B: If they need more proof or a lower barrier*

"Here's what I'd suggest as a first step: a Growth Audit.

One week, I'll analyze your current pipeline end-to-end — sources, conversion rates, leak points, competitive positioning — and deliver a 90-day growth roadmap.

$497. If you're not impressed, you get a full refund.

Worth starting there?"

---

**OBJECTIONS (Throughout)**

| If they say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "It's too expensive" | "I understand. What would it cost you to have another slow quarter? Let's compare that to $3,500." |
| "We're too busy" | "That's exactly why you need a system. The busier you are delivering, the less time you have to prospect — which means the feast/famine cycle gets worse." |
| "We tried this before" | "I appreciate that. What specifically didn't work? [Listen]. Here's how our approach is different: [specific difference]." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically needs more thought? Let's address that now so you have everything you need." |
| "Send me a proposal" | "I'd love to. To make sure it's accurate, let me ask one more question: [qualifying question]. Great. I'll send it by [day/time]. When should I follow up?" |
| "We're doing fine with referrals" | "That's great — referrals are the best source of business. The question is: are you getting as many as you could? The firms we work with keep their referral channel AND add a systematic outbound channel on top." |

---

**CLOSE (25-28 min)**

"Based on everything you've shared, here's what I recommend:"

[Summarize your recommendation — Audit or Retainer]

"I'll send you a [proposal/next steps] by [day/time].

What questions do you have before we move forward?"

[Handle any remaining objections]

"Great. I'll send that over. [Brief personal commitment — e.g., 'I'll have it in your inbox by tomorrow morning.']

[Name], appreciate the conversation. Talk soon."

---

**POST-CALL (28-30 min)**

1. Send calendar invite for next touchpoint
2. Send proposal or audit offer within 24 hours
3. Connect on LinkedIn with personalized note
4. Log everything in CRM
5. Add to follow-up sequence

## Script 2: Proposal Follow-up Call (20 minutes)

**Purpose:** Convert proposal to signed client
**Best for:** 5-7 days after proposal sent

---

**OPENING**

"Hi [Name], thanks for circling back. I sent over the proposal on [day] — wanted to see what questions have come up."

**IF THEY'VE READ IT:**
"What stood out to you? What questions do you have?"

[Listen. Address each question directly.]

"Does the proposed timeline work for you?"

"Can we talk through the next steps to get started?"

**IF THEY HAVEN'T READ IT:**
"No problem at all — I know you're busy. Let me walk through the highlights quickly."

[2-minute summary of proposal]

"What's your instinct so far?"

"But more importantly — how does this feel relative to the problem we discussed on our last call? Does this solution address it?"

**IF THEY'RE HESITATING:**
"What's the main thing holding you back?"

[Listen carefully — the real objection is usually hidden]

"Let me ask you this: if you knew for certain this would work — that in 90 days you'd have 40% more pipeline — would you move forward today?"

— If yes: The barrier is risk. Offer guarantee or trial.
— If no: The barrier is something else. Probe deeper.

**IF THEY'RE READY:**
"Great. Let me send you the agreement. One signature and we're live.

When would you like to start — next Monday or the following?"

## Script 3: Monthly Business Review (45 minutes)

**Purpose:** Show value, align priorities, get referrals
**Best for:** Active retainer clients

---

**AGENDA**

| Time | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-5 | Wins this month |
| 5-15 | Pipeline review — metrics vs targets |
| 15-25 | What's working / what's not |
| 25-35 | Strategy for next month |
| 35-45 | Opportunities + referrals |

**SCRIPTS BY SECTION**

**Wins (0-5 min)**
"Let's start with wins. What went well this month?"

[Let them celebrate first]

"From our side: [share 2-3 specific wins — meetings booked, replies received, pipeline growth]."

**Pipeline Review (5-15 min)**
"Here's where we landed this month:"

[Share dashboard: new leads, meetings, proposals, closed]

"We're [ahead/behind] target. Here's why I think that is: [analysis]."

"One thing I want to flag: [specific insight or concern]."

**What's Working/Not Working (15-25 min)**
"What's working well in our process? What should we do more of?"

"What's not working? What should we change?"

"Based on what I'm seeing, I'd suggest we [specific recommendation]."

**Strategy (25-35 min)**
"Next month, our priorities should be:"

1. [Priority 1 — e.g., scale LinkedIn outreach]
2. [Priority 2 — e.g., refine messaging for top prospect segment]
3. [Priority 3 — e.g., follow up on stalled opportunities]

"How does that sound?"

**Referrals (35-45 min)**
"Last thing: who do you know who's facing similar growth challenges?"

"I'm not looking for a hard sell — just an introduction. And if they become a client, I'll send you $1,000."

"Even one name would help."

## Script 4: Cold Call (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Get a meeting
**Best for:** Warm leads, follow-up after email

---

**OPENING**

"Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] with [Your Company]. I know I'm catching you cold — I'll be brief.

I help professional services firms build predictable pipeline systems so they don't have to rely on referrals alone.

Quick question: is inconsistent pipeline a challenge for you right now?"

**IF YES:**
"Great — that's exactly what I specialize in. I'd love to show you a framework we use.

Do you have 15 minutes this [day] or [day] to hop on a quick call?"

**IF NO / "WE'RE FINE":**
"Glad to hear it. Most firms I talk to say referrals are their main source — and that works until it doesn't.

Two questions:
1. What happens if your top referrer goes quiet for a quarter?
2. Would you be open to a 10-minute conversation about building a backup system — just in case?"

**IF "SEND ME INFO":**
"I'd love to. But 5 minutes on the phone would tell me exactly what's relevant for you.

What's better — Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am?"

**IF NOT INTERESTED:**
"Fair enough. I'll send you a quick email with a framework you might find useful — no further follow-up unless you ask."

## Script 5: Retention / At-Risk Client (15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Prevent churn
**Best for:** Client showing signs of disengagement (missed calls, slow response, pipeline stalled)

---

**OPENING**

"Hi [Name], I wanted to check in. I've noticed [specific behavior — e.g., we've missed a couple calls / pipeline has been quiet].

I want to make sure we're still delivering value. How are you feeling about our work together?"

**IF POSITIVE:**
"Glad to hear it. Let's talk about what we need to adjust to keep momentum going."

[Reset priorities, recommit to process]

**IF NEUTRAL / HESITANT:**
"I appreciate your honesty. What's not working?"

[Listen carefully. Common issues: slow results, unclear ROI, budget pressure]

[Address directly:
- Slow results: "Pipeline building takes 60-90 days to show. We're in week [X]. Here's what I expect to see in the next 30 days."
- Unclear ROI: "Let me pull together a results report showing exactly what we've delivered."
- Budget: "Let's talk about adjusting scope or timing to fit your current budget."]

**IF NEGATIVE:**
"I hear you. Let's figure out the right path forward — whether that's adjusting our approach or ending on good terms."

[If they want to leave: End gracefully, ask for feedback, leave door open for future.]

## Script 6: Referral Ask (2 minutes)

**Purpose:** Get warm introductions
**Best for:** At peak satisfaction moments

---

**IN-PERSON / PHONE:**
"[Client Name], I've really enjoyed working with you and I'm proud of the results we've achieved together.

I'm trying to grow my business through referrals — and you're exactly the type of client I'd love more of.

Do you know 2-3 other firm leaders who face similar challenges to what you had before we started working together?"

**EMAIL:**
"Subject: Quick favor

Hi [Name],

Quick ask: do you know 2-3 other firm leaders who face similar growth challenges to what you had before we started working together?

I'm not looking for a formal introduction — just names and companies. If they become a client, I'll send you $1,000.

Even one name would mean a lot.

Thanks!"
